## 2. Chapter 4 drafting doctrine (AUTHOR LOCK — 2026-06-08)

These objectives **override stylistic discretion** for all Ch. 4 prose. They refine — never erase — prior governance in `LOCK-MANIFEST.md`, `SUBSECTION-DRAFTING-CONTRACT-ULTIMATE.md`, and `GCHECK-MASTER-CUMULATIVE.md`.

### 2.1 Central thesis (inevitability arc)

Chapter 4 closes Volume I with one consequence already implicit in Chapters 1–3:

> **Representation is coordinate freedom; realizability is geometry-imposed rank.**

The chapter must read as though spectral truncation, coupling limits, and effective electromagnetic dimension were **forced** by finite support, finite aperture, and the radiation operator chain — not introduced as a new topic. Every section advances that single arc. A timeless monograph makes the reader feel the conclusion was always there; Ch. 4 supplies the proof architecture.

### 2.2 Peer register (assume intelligence)

| Do | Do not |
|----|--------|
| Write for a research peer who has read Ch. 1–3 | Write for a student encountering operators for the first time |
| State assumptions once, then derive | Re-teach Maxwell, Hilbert space, or VSH |
| Cite prior results by `\ref{...}` / equation number and proceed | Explain why re-derivation is omitted |
| Let equations carry the argument | Over-explain correctness of steps the reader can verify |
| Use precise new terms where Ch. 4 owns the concept | Pad with survey, hype, or motivational filler |

**Banned prose registers:** “Students should note …”, “It is important to remember …”, “We will not repeat …”, “As established in the inheritance section …”, “This subsection establishes …”.

\section{Introduction and Scope}
\label{sec:ch10}

Chapter~\ref{ch:01} introduces the conceptual framework used throughout the
monograph. The aim at this stage is to place the central question in clear
mathematical and physical terms before proceeding to detailed operator and
spectral developments. Section~\ref{sec:ch101} describes the scope and role of
the chapter, Section~\ref{sec:ch102} presents angular structure as a consequence
of Maxwell dynamics under symmetry and boundary conditions, and
Section~\ref{sec:ch103} specifies the admissibility class and validity domain
for the results that follow. This sequence provides a coherent foundation for
reading the subsequent derivations with appropriate assumptions and
interpretation.


\subsection{Scope and role of Chapter 1}
\label{sec:ch101}
% TOC tag: [HOME]

This monograph is written for advanced graduate students, doctoral researchers,
and practicing scientists or engineers who already command Maxwell theory and
vector analysis, and who require a proof-structured treatment of angular
electromagnetic organization \cite{jackson1999,harrington2001,collin2001}. The
chapter mandate is not to compile device narratives; it is to establish the
physical and mathematical language under which later statements are judged
admissible, realizable, and observable.

Chapter~\ref{ch:01} has a precise foundational function within Volume~I. It
establishes, in one place, the statements that subsequent chapters will invoke
without re-derivation: Maxwellian field structure in the adopted phasor
convention and SI system, conservation relations for energy and momentum
transport, and the distinction between representational descriptors and
operational observables. The chapter is therefore not an orientation survey; it
is the formal base from which later operator and spectral developments are read.

The assumptions adopted here are classical and explicit. The analysis remains
within Maxwell electrodynamics with admissible sources, admissible boundary
data, and the time convention \(\exp(-\jj\omega t)\). The intended reader is
expected to possess graduate-level preparation, yet the exposition remains
derivationally complete at each theorem-bearing step so that inferential
continuity is preserved and technical conclusions are traceable to stated
premises.

Its scope boundary is equally deliberate. This chapter does not construct the
full operator-domain program developed in Chapter~2, the eigenfield
representation machinery developed in Chapter~3, or the finite-geometry
realizability limits developed in Chapter~4. Those subjects are treated at
their designated proof homes and then inherited forward by reference, in
accordance with the forward progression architecture of the manuscript.

Within these limits, the chapter clarifies the working meaning of angular
electromagnetic structure: directional organization, rotational covariance, and
angular-momentum content are interpreted through conservation-governed field
quantities and measurement procedures, rather than through nomenclature based on
phase pattern alone.

This scope statement leads directly to Section~\ref{sec:ch102}, where the
origin of angular structure is developed from Maxwell symmetry and boundary
constraints.


\subsection{Angular structure from Maxwell symmetry and boundary conditions}
\label{sec:ch102}
% TOC tag: [HOME]

Angular structure is introduced here through Maxwell boundary-value dynamics
rather than through an a priori catalogue of modal labels
\cite{jackson1999,harrington2001}. Let \(\Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^3\) denote the
analysis region, \(\Gamma=\partial\Omega\) its boundary, \(\chi\) the
constitutive descriptor of the medium model, and \(\Jimp\) the impressed
electric current density. Under these declarations, the phasor fields are
written as
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch1.maxwell-map}
(\E,\H)=\mathcal{M}_{\Omega,\Gamma,\chi}\!\left[\Jimp\right],
\end{equation}
where \(\mathcal{M}_{\Omega,\Gamma,\chi}\) denotes the Maxwell map from
admissible source data to admissible field pairs. Equation
\eqref{eq:ch1.maxwell-map} assigns angular organization to the image structure
of a constrained operator, not to coordinate naming conventions. Physically,
Eq.~\eqref{eq:ch1.maxwell-map} places angular behavior within the same causal
framework that governs electromagnetic transport and exchange
\cite{jackson1999,collin2001}.

To separate transport observables from representation coordinates, define
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch1.angular-observable-map}
\mathcal{A}(\E,\H)=\big(\rho_J,\Phi_J,\Sigma\big),
\end{equation}
where \(\rho_J\) denotes an angular-momentum density observable, \(\Phi_J\)
denotes angular-momentum flux across a specified surface, and \(\Sigma\) denotes
chosen angular representation coefficients. Equation
\eqref{eq:ch1.angular-observable-map} distinguishes three non-equivalent levels
of description: local storage through \(\rho_J\), transported content through
\(\Phi_J\), and representational decomposition through \(\Sigma\). The
operational interpretation follows directly from
Eq.~\eqref{eq:ch1.angular-observable-map}: phase structure alone is not taken as
evidence of transported angular momentum unless flux and conservation accounting
support that conclusion \cite{jackson1999,harrington2001}.

The symmetry consequence can now be stated in compact form. When the effective
Maxwell map commutes with active rotational action, rotationally adapted field
structure is preserved; when this commutation is broken by geometry,
constitutive anisotropy, or boundary restriction, angular coupling and spectral
redistribution appear as structural consequences \cite{harrington2001,collin2001}.
In Chapter~\ref{ch:01}, this result is established at principle level; rigorous
operator proofs and representation-level elaboration are deferred to their HOME
developments in later chapters.


\subsection{Assumptions, finite-energy admissibility, and domain of validity}
\label{sec:ch103}
% TOC tag: [HOME]

The present chapter is formulated on an admissible finite-energy field class.
For the declared analysis region \(\Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^3\), the working
condition is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch1.energy-class}
\int_{\Omega}\!\left(\E^*\!\cdot\E+\H^*\!\cdot\H\right)\,dV<\infty,
\end{equation}
where \((\cdot)^*\) denotes complex conjugation, and \(dV\) is the volume
element on \(\Omega\). Equation~\eqref{eq:ch1.energy-class} identifies the
function class in which field inner products, norm-based estimates, and later
projection arguments are mathematically well-posed. At the level of physical
interpretation, Eq.~\eqref{eq:ch1.energy-class} excludes infinite-energy
idealizations from the admissible state space and thereby enforces realizable
electromagnetic modeling \cite{jackson1999,collin2001}.

The accompanying assumptions are fixed throughout Chapter~\ref{ch:01}: SI unit
normalization, phasor convention \(\exp(-\jj\omega t)\) with \(\omega>0\), and
linear local constitutive closure unless an explicit exception is introduced in
a later HOME section. Every symbol is defined at first appearance and used in
the notation regime locked for Volume~I.

The validity boundary is therefore explicit and non-ambiguous. Strongly
nonlinear response, active gain media, rapidly time-varying constitutive laws,
and quantum-field corrections are outside the theorem scope of this chapter.
Such regimes are referenced only where their formal treatment is assigned in the
forward map \cite{harrington2001,jackson1999}. This admissibility statement
completes Subsection~\ref{sec:ch103} and provides the closure required for the
Section~\ref{sec:ch10} bridge that follows.

of angular structure, and declared the admissibility regime under which

proceeds to Section~\ref{sec:ch11}, where Maxwell equations and conservation
laws are developed as the first formal backbone of the monograph.

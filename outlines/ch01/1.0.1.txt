# §1.0.1 Scope and role of Chapter 1 — author outline (LOCKED input)

## 1. Audience
- Advanced graduate students, doctoral researchers, practicing scientists, engineers
- Fields: electromagnetics, optics, antennas, wave physics, applied mathematics
- Prerequisites: Maxwell equations, vector calculus, linear algebra, PDEs, classical EM
- Develops angular structure from first principles: rigorous + physically unified

## 2. Volume I (Ch. 1–4) — monograph context
- Vol. I: physical, mathematical, operator-theoretic foundations
- Topics across Vol. I (not all in Ch. 1): conservation/AM transport; rotation/gauge; measurement;
  radiation operators; Green functions; eigenfields; VSH; realizability; truncation
- Objective: what angular structure is admissible, realizable, observable before mechanisms/apps
- Vol. II (Ch. 5+): radiation mechanisms, arrays, apertures, metasurfaces, propagation, matter — cite only here

## 3. Angular structure (one idea for Ch. 1 intro)
- Complete organization of a field w.r.t. direction, rotational symmetry, AM content, angular spectrum — Maxwell-governed

## 4. Chapter 1 only — deferred to Ch. 2–4
- No: operator theory, Green mappings, Hilbert space, SO(3)/AM operators, VSH, completeness/normalization,
  truncation/realizability/scaling (Ch. 4)
- Ch. 1: physical principles, conservation, symmetry, gauge, observability

## 5. Notation
- exp(-j omega t) phasors; SI; symbols defined at first formal appearance

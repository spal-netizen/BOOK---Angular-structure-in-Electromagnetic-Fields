# Model Workflow Lock (Execution Policy)

Status: LOCKED for seamless drafting.

## Answer to capability question

Sonnet is strong for technical writing and mathematical exposition, but for this project's strict "legendary monograph" standard, Sonnet should be used for fast drafting passes, not as the final rigor gate on theorem-heavy text.

## Locked model roles

1. Opus role (final rigor authority):
   - theorem-bearing subsections,
   - major derivations and proof chains,
   - final pass before accepting subsection as complete.

2. Sonnet role (high-throughput drafting):
   - first-pass expansion from TOC bullets,
   - section transitions and narrative flow drafts,
   - intermediate refinement before final rigor pass.

3. Codex/GPT coding role (infrastructure and enforcement):
   - repository edits, scripts, build tools, linting,
   - governance lock updates, consistency automation,
   - formatting and workflow operations.

## Switching policy

Model switching is allowed and encouraged by task type.
Switching is mandatory when moving from drafting speed tasks to proof-critical acceptance tasks.

## Completion gate (non-negotiable)

A subsection is "complete" only after:

- theorem-home compliance check,
- no-recursion audit checks,
- citation contract checks,
- final Opus-level rigor pass on mathematical and physical explanation quality.

## Practical sequence per subsection

1. Draft (Sonnet).
2. Technical enforcement and structure checks (Codex/GPT coding model).
3. Final acceptance pass (Opus).

This lock applies to all 19 chapters.
